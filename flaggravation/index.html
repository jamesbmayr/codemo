Flaggravation

setup

about

This is a vexillological drafting card game with a score-keeping web app.

Players collect components common to flags from all around the world - like colors, structures, and objects often featured on flags - and use these to claim flags shown on screen.

cards

To download and print the cards, visit Flaggravation on DreamHatcher Games.

rules

setup

  1. Open the Flaggravation web app on a phone or tablet.
    • Enter the names of all players.
    • Set a Flag Goal. For first-time players, the recommended goal is 5.
    • Click "Continue". Place the device in a central spot close to all players.
  2. Spread out all the component cards, face-down, in a messy Pool on the table.
  3. Each player selects a Hand of 4 random cards from the Pool.

turn order

Play is simultaneous each round.

gameplay

  1. All players pick up 1 random card from the Pool to add to their Hand, for 5 total cards.
  2. All players select 1 card from their Hand to place face-up in front of them in a Personal Pile. At any time, you may reorganize your Personal Pile.
  3. Once all players have made a selection, all players pass the remaining 4 cards in their Hand to the left (clockwise).
  4. Play continues in this way throughout the game.

special rules

  1. At any point, if, in your Personal Pile in front of you, you have the components required for a Flag shown on the app, say the name of the Flag aloud.
    • Some cards are "Wild" and represent any 1 of a set of components. (Ex: Colors substitutes for a Red, Yellow, Green, Blue, Black, or White.)
    • If 2 players have a claim (see below), it's whoever says the Flag name first.
  2. Reveal these component cards to show they match the Flag's requirements.
    • If they do not, place these cards back in your Personal Pile.
    • If they do meet the requirements:
      1. Place these component cards in a face-up Discard Deck to the side.
      2. The app shows the number of cards to collect next to the flag. Add this quantity of random Pool cards to your Personal Pile.
      3. Select the applicable Flag, and then your name, to increase your score.
      4. Switch the direction of passing (clockwise vs. counterclockwise) until the next Flag is claimed.
  3. When the Pool is exhausted, shuffle the Discard Deck and create a new Pool.

end

The game ends when someone has claimed the Goal number of Flags; this player wins.
